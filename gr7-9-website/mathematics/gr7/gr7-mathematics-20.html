Functions and relationships

1. Use the formula for the area of a rectangle (\(A = l \times b)\) to calculate the following:

(a) The area, if the length is 0,4 m and the breadth is 0,3 m

(b) The length, if the area is 12,4 cm2 and the breadth is 4 cm

(c) The breadth, if the area is 14,4 m2 and the length is 12 m

2. The formula for the total surface area of a cylinder of base radius \(r\) and height \(h\) is:\(A = 2 \pi r ^2 + 2 \pi rh\)

\(\pi\) or pi (pronounced "pie") is a

number approximately equal to \(\frac{22}{7}\).

You will learn about pi in Grade 8.

Use this formula to calculate the surface area if \(\pi = \frac{22}{7}\), the base radius is 7 cm and the height is 3 cm.

3. The formula for finding the temperature in degrees Centigrade (°C) is \(C = \frac{22}{7} (F- 32)\), where F is the temperature in degrees Fahrenheit (°F). What is the temperature in °C if it is 59 °F?
