Making patterns from rules

1. (a) Start at 30. Add -5 and write the answer. Add -5 again and write the answer. Continue until you have a number sequence with 10 terms.

(b) Start at -30. Add -5 and write the answer. Add -5 again and write the answer. Continue until you have a number sequence with 10 terms.

(c) Start at -30. Add 5 and write the answer. Add 5 again and write the answer. Continue until you have a number sequence with 10 terms.

2. (a) The first term of a sequence is -10 and there is a constant difference of 5 between the terms. Write down the first ten terms of the sequence.

(b) The first term of a sequence is -10 and there is a constant difference of -5 between the terms. Write down the first ten terms of the sequence.

3. Choose a number to be your first term and another number to be a constant difference. Write the first ten terms of your sequence.

4. Choose a number smaller than -10 to be your first term and another number to be a constant difference. Write the first ten terms of your sequence.

6. Choose a negative number to be your first term and another negative number to be a constant difference. Write the first ten terms of your sequence.

7. Choose a number to be your tenth term and another number to be a constant difference. Write the first ten terms of your sequence.

3. A certain pattern is formed by a common difference of 6.

(a) What number follows on 23 in this pattern?

(b) What number is followed by 23 in this pattern?

(c) What number follows on 47 in this pattern?

(d) What number is followed by 47 in this pattern?

Consider the sequence: 10 6 2 -2 -6 . . . . . . . . . In this sequence, 2 follows on 6. They are called consecutive terms.

When one number follows another in a sequence they are called consecutive terms.
