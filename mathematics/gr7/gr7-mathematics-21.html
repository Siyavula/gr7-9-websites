Assessment

In this section, the numbers in brackets at the end of a question indicate the number of marks that the question is worth. Use this information to help you determine how much working is needed.

The total number of marks allocated to the assessment is 50.

(a) 28,3; 31,1; 33,9; ...

(b) \(\frac{2}{5}; \frac{6}{5}; \frac{18}{5}\)

3. (a) If \(a = b^3 - (2c^2 + 8)\), what is the value of \(a\) if \(b = 5\) and \(c = -4?\)

(b) Use the formula \(p = \frac{4s-a^2}{t}\) to calculate the value of \(t\) if \(p = 36\), \(s = 45\)and \(a = 6\)

4. A soccer ball costs x rand. Write down in terms of x the costs of the following items:

(a) five soccer balls

(b) a rugby ball that costs twice as much as the soccer ball

(c) a dress that costs R150 more than the soccer ball

(d) three soccer balls and a dress

5. Solve for x:

(a) \(x + 18 = 52 \)

(b) \(5x = 60\)

(c) \( \frac{x}{3} = 12\)

6. You are given that 2x + 8 = 15. Write down the value of 2x + 10.

7. Study the following graph, showing the average minimum temperatures in Cape Town, and then answer the questions that follow:

 

(a) Describe the trend in the minimum temperatures from July to December.

(b) Which month has the lowest minimum temperature, and what is the average minimum temperature in this month?

(c) Write down the names of the month(s) with the highest minimum temperatures, and their average minimum temperatures.

8. Draw graphs that fit each of the following descriptions. Remember to label the axes. You don't have to show any values on the axes.

(a) A graph for the total cost of different weights of stewing beef

(b) A speed-time graph for a vehicle that is accelerating
